Now what is Calorie Surplus ?

Calories are the units of energy you get from foods and beverages, and when you consume more calories than you burn, you achieve a calorie surplus. Though calorie calculators provide a good idea of your maintenance calorie needs, you can get a more precise number by tracking your calorie intake and weight for 10 days.

Now let us list down the steps to bulk up the weight:

1. SETTING UP YOUR CALORIES:
Your weight may fluctuate day to day, but if your weight has otherwise remained stable over the 10 days, the average number of calories you consumed per day is a better representation of your maintenance calories.

2. STARTING POINT TO BULK UP:
Experts recommend consuming 10–20% above your daily weight maintenance calorie needs during the bulking phase for an average weight gain of 0.25–0.5% of your body weight per week. If someone consumes more than 30% of your maintainance calories then they will be gaining more body fat percentage in their body. To avoid this experts recommend to follow a lean bulking process rather than dirty bulk. After that if the weight does not increase further then either the person has to further increse the daily calorie consumption or decrease the cardio sessions.

What is Dirty Bulk?
Dirty Bulking is a process of gaining weight by putting themselves into calorie surplus more than that needed for a lean bulk. Just as many people find it difficult to lose weight, others find it hard to gain weight. For these individuals, a dirty bulk may be a helpful tool, as it usually promotes a significant calorie surplus and thus leads to weight gain.

3. SPLITTING UP OUR MACROS:
According to fitness science'
45–60% of your calories must be filled up with carbs. 30–35% of your calories must be filled up with protein. 15–30% of your calories must be filled up with fat.

4.TRAINING AND CARDIO:
As you gain weight, your maintenance calories will increase over time, and you will need to adjust your calorie intake based on your weight gain goals.When bulking, it’s easy to eat high calorie, nutrient-poor foods like desserts or fried foods to rapidly achieve a calorie surplus. Yet, healthy bulking is possible as long as you focus on nutrient-dense foods. Cardio is important for everyone to increase their stamina. So it doesn't mean that no cardio is required when someone is on a bulk.

5.TRACK YOUR PROGRESS:
Four things that everyone needs to track during their weightloss journey are,
* Your calorie intake.
* Your protien intake.
* Calories burned.
* Track your body weight. Use the same scale, at the same time of day, and wearing the same clothes.
