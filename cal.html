Calories

What Is a Calorie?

A calorie is an exact unit of energy, specifically, it is the amount of energy needed to raise the temperature of 1 gram (g) of water by 1 degree Celsius. The “calories” in food are actually kilocalories, or 1,000 of these tiny units. Fats are the most calorie-dense foods we eat, with about 9 kilocalories (kcal) per gram. Carbohydrates and protein each have about 4 kcal per gram. On the most basic level, eating fewer calories than you burn will result in weight loss.

What are High-Calorie-Dense Foods?

High-Calorie-dense foods are foods that contain high amounts of calories relative to their weight.These kinds of foods can be consumed to gain weight.

What are Low-Calorie-Dense Foods?

Low-Calorie-dense foods are foods that contain less amounts of calories relative to their weight. These kinds of foods can be consumed to lose weight.
